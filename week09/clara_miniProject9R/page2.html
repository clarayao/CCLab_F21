What does this poem convey?

  • This is a poem about death.

  • The near death of Dylan's father makes him think about death in a more general way, in which he thinks about how five different types of men would react in the face of death, and establishes these ideas in the 2-6 stanzas of the poem.

  • In the end, Dylan realizes that death cannot be avoided, but it can be challenged.

  • He thus wants to use this poem to encourage his father, as well as the others that are stuggling, to always "rage" in the face of death or even just a tiny struggle.
